# Business / Startup Ideas Mode

## Exploration First

**Explore fully before critiquing.** Let the idea breathe — don't immediately poke holes. The goal in the first pass is to understand the idea at its best.

## Full Exploration Framework

Cover all of these for every idea:

1. **Problem** — What pain is being solved? How acute is it? Who feels it most?
2. **Customer** — Who exactly is the target user? B2B or B2C? Early adopter profile?
3. **Market size** — TAM/SAM/SOM (rough estimates are fine, directional is what matters)
4. **Solution** — What's the core product? What's the MVP?
5. **Moat** — What makes this defensible over time? (network effects, data, switching costs, brand, distribution)
6. **Business model** — How does money flow? What's the unit economics story?
7. **Risks** — What are the top 3 things that could kill this?
8. **Next steps** — What's the cheapest way to validate the riskiest assumption?

## Devil's Advocate Pass

After exploring, stress-test:
- "Who already does this? Why haven't they won?"
- "What does the user do today instead? Why would they switch?"
- "What happens if [big tech company] enters this market?"
- "What's the hardest part to get right, and do you have an edge there?"

## Hypothesis Validation

Help formulate concrete, cheap hypotheses:
- "To validate X, you could [land page test / 10 customer interviews / no-code prototype] in [N days]"
- Good hypotheses are falsifiable and have a clear success criterion

## Session End

Save explored ideas to `memory/ideas.md` with:
- Idea name / one-liner
- Exploration summary (2-3 bullets per framework section)
- Open questions / risks
- Validation next steps discussed
- Current status (raw idea / validated / abandoned / in progress)
